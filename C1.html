The Problem

Knowledge Gap

SonoGuar has been validated for image quality and physician preference, but no study has quantified what adoption would actually save a training program or the U.S. medical education system as a whole. Without a dollar figure, administrators cannot make a funding case — and the gel stays in the lab. A credible economic model translates a proof-of-concept into a policy argument.

Central Hypothesis

"Nationwide adoption of SonoGuar as the primary ultrasound phantom material would generate seven-figure annual savings across U.S. residency and medical school programs, with individual programs saving $1,000–$15,000 per year depending on size and procedure volume."

Methods

🔢6-Step Protocol

  • 1
    Program Census
    Pull ACGME.org program counts for EM, IM, FM, Critical Care, Anesthesia. Estimate annual US simulation sessions per specialty from published curricula.
    Source: ACGME.org / GME Data Resource Book
  • 2
    Commercial Phantom Pricing
    Catalog list prices from Blue Phantom, Simulab, Kyoto Kagaku, ElevateHealth. Document lifespan claims and replacement frequency from product pages.
    Sources: kyotokagaku.com · elevatehealth.net · bluephantom.com
  • 3
    SonoGuar Cost Build-up
    Itemize ingredients (guar gum, borax, glycerol, canola oil) from bulk and grocery retail. Add mold amortization, labor time at $20/hr, consumable balloons at $0.06 each.
    From: Bikkani et al. 2026 supplementary materials
  • 4
    Per-Program Model
    Build a single Excel decision tree for one program. Vary insert lifespan (1–5 sessions), sessions/year, program size. Calculate annual commercial vs. SonoGuar cost.
    Tool: Microsoft Excel / Google Sheets
  • 5
    National Scale-Up
    Multiply per-program savings by ACGME program counts at 10%, 25%, 50% adoption rates. Report range as pessimistic / base / optimistic scenarios.
    Adoption scenarios: 10% / 25% / 50%
  • 6
    Sensitivity Analysis
    One-way and two-way sensitivity on the 5 highest-impact variables using Excel Data Table. Present results as a tornado diagram.
    Output: tornado diagram + break-even insert lifespan

💵Cost Formula

// Per-program annual cost
Annual_Cost =
  (sessions × inserts_per_session × cost_per_insert)
  + (sessions × inserts_per_session × balloon_cost)
  + (mold_cost / mold_lifespan_yr)

// National aggregate
National_Savings =
  Σ (Commercial_Cost − SonoGuar_Cost)
    × programs × adoption_rate

All inputs are published or publicly available. No proprietary data required.

⚠Pitfalls & Fixes

  • ⚠️
    No published insert lifespan for SonoGuar
    Fix: Use a conservative 1-session lifespan as primary analysis; run sensitivity up to 5 sessions. State limitation explicitly.
  • ⚠️
    Commercial phantom prices vary widely
    Fix: Use publicly listed prices; document retrieval dates. Treat price as a sensitivity variable with a $200–$500 range.
  • ⚠️
    ACGME data only shows program counts, not session frequency
    Fix: Derive sessions/year from published ACGME milestone requirements and EM curriculum literature.
  • ⚠️
    Reviewers may question model oversimplification
    Fix: Frame explicitly as a conservative first-estimate model. Acknowledge excluded costs (storage, QC) as future work.
