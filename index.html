How Each Study Works
C1 · Economic Model
1
Collect data — ACGME program counts + commercial phantom prices from manufacturer websites
2
Build cost formula — SonoGuar ingredients itemized from Bikkani et al.; commercial from list prices
3
Excel decision tree — per-program annual cost at 10%, 25%, 50% adoption scenarios
4
Sensitivity analysis — tornado diagram testing insert lifespan, sessions/year, phantom price
5
Scale nationally — multiply per-program savings across all U.S. residency programs
6
Write & submit — brief report, 1,500–2,500 words, CHEERS 2022 checklist
C2 · Patient Safety Model
1
Pull published inputs — CDC NHSN CLABSI rates, AHRQ cost data, Barsuk 2014 effect size
2
Establish baseline — calculate current U.S. CLABSIs, deaths, and total cost per year
3
Apply SBML effect — model CLABSI rate reduction at conservative 30–45% (published: up to 84%)
4
Excel decision tree — CLABSIs prevented, deaths averted, cost savings, ROI per scenario
5
Sensitivity analysis — SBML effect size and adoption rate are the dominant variables
6
Write & submit — lead with CLABSI burden, not phantoms; frame as patient safety paper
C3 · Environmental LCA
1
Define scope — functional unit: 1 training session; system boundary: cradle-to-grave
2
SonoGuar inventory — itemize ingredient weights from Bikkani et al.; link to ecoinvent database
3
Commercial inventory — estimate PVC/polyurethane composition; model shipping and lifespan
4
Run OpenLCA — free software; output CO₂-eq, plastic waste, and water use per session
5
Scale nationally — convert to EPA equivalencies (trees, car miles) for public communication
6
Write & submit — lead with healthcare's 4.4% GHG share; ISO 14044 reporting standard
Source publication: Bikkani et al., Western Journal of Emergency Medicine, 2026.
